## First Impression

[Medspa Name] has a strong service offering, but the online presence could do more to build instant trust and make it easier for new clients to understand what to book first.

The biggest opportunity is to make the website and social content feel more guided: what services are best for which concerns, what results are realistic, and why this team is the right choice.

## Website Quick Wins

1. Make the primary booking CTA more visible above the fold.
2. Add a clearer "Start Here" service path for new clients.
3. Add more trust signals near service pages: testimonials, practitioner credentials, FAQs, and before/after proof where appropriate.
4. Rewrite the homepage headline to focus on the client outcome, not just the service list.
5. Add a simple lead capture offer, such as "Not sure what to book? Take our 60-second treatment quiz."

## Social Quick Wins

1. Pin three posts: "Start Here," "Most Booked Treatments," and "Real Results / Client Love."
2. Turn FAQs into weekly short-form videos.
3. Add more human/team content so the business feels warmer and more trustworthy.
4. Create a recurring "Treatment Myth vs. Fact" series.
5. Use more booking-focused CTAs instead of only general awareness captions.

## Suggested Content Pillars

### Education

Goal: Help clients understand which treatment is right for them.

Examples:
- "Botox vs. filler: what is the difference?"
- "3 signs you might be ready for [treatment]"
- "What to know before your first medspa appointment"

### Trust

Goal: Reduce hesitation.

Examples:
- Practitioner intro posts
- Client testimonials
- Treatment room walk-through
- Safety/credential content

### Results

Goal: Show outcomes without overpromising.

Examples:
- Before/after content where compliant
- "What results look like after 2 weeks"
- "A subtle refresh, not a totally different face"

### Behind The Scenes

Goal: Make the business feel personal.

Examples:
- Morning setup
- Meet the injector
- Product shelf favorites
- Treatment prep

### Seasonal / Local

Goal: Create urgency.

Examples:
- Wedding season skin prep
- Summer glow-up treatments
- Holiday party refresh
- Winter skin repair

## Sample UGC-Style Video Concepts

### Concept 1: "I Was Nervous To Book"

Hook:
"I was nervous to try [treatment], so I found a medspa that actually explains everything first."

Visual:
Walk-in, consultation moment, treatment room, product shelf, soft after shot.

CTA:
"Book a consultation if you are not sure where to start."

### Concept 2: "What I Wish I Knew Before"

Hook:
"Three things I wish I knew before my first medspa appointment."

Body:
- You do not need to know exactly what to book.
- A good provider should explain your options.
- Subtle results are often the goal.

CTA:
"Save this before your first appointment."

### Concept 3: "Treatment Matchmaker"

Hook:
"If your concern is [concern], here is where I would start."

Body:
Match 3 common concerns to 3 services.

CTA:
"DM or book a consult for help choosing."

### Concept 4: "Local Find"

Hook:
"A local spot for a subtle refresh without feeling overdone."

Visual:
Exterior, front desk, team, service room, detail shots.

CTA:
"Save this for your next self-care appointment."

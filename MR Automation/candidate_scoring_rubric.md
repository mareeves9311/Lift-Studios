# Candidate Scoring Rubric

Use this to decide whether a business is worth outreach.

Score each lead from 0-20. Prioritize anything 13+.

## 1. Revenue Potential: 0-4

- 4: High-ticket service, consult-based, recurring, or premium client value
- 3: Medium-ticket service with clear booking/inquiry value
- 2: Lower-ticket service but still appointment-based
- 1: Unclear value per customer
- 0: No obvious monetization path

Examples of 4-point niches:

- Med spas
- Cosmetic dental
- Interior design
- Wedding services
- Premium wellness clinics

## 2. Website Weakness: 0-4

- 4: Clearly outdated, confusing, poor mobile UX, weak CTA, or broken-looking pages
- 3: Functional but generic, cluttered, weak messaging, or hard-to-book
- 2: Decent site with a few fixable issues
- 1: Strong site with minor polish opportunities
- 0: Excellent site; probably not worth pitching

## 3. Conversion Path: 0-4

- 4: Site should clearly drive booking, consults, calls, purchases, or inquiries
- 3: Conversion path exists but could be easier
- 2: Conversion path is present but low urgency
- 1: Hard to identify what action visitors should take
- 0: No meaningful action to improve

## 4. Business Credibility: 0-4

- 4: Active reviews, active Instagram, visible team/services, premium photos, signs of demand
- 3: Some credibility signals but inconsistent presentation
- 2: Basic business presence, limited proof
- 1: Very little proof of active business
- 0: Looks inactive or abandoned

## 5. Ease Of Personalization: 0-4

- 4: Obvious specific fixes can be named in 30 seconds
- 3: A few clear fixes visible from homepage/services page
- 2: Generic improvement possible but not highly specific
- 1: Hard to personalize without deeper research
- 0: No clear angle

## Outreach Priority

- 17-20: A-list lead. Send personalized audit or Loom.
- 13-16: Good lead. Send personalized cold email.
- 9-12: Maybe. Use lower-effort outreach only.
- 0-8: Skip.

## Green Flags

- Expensive service menu
- Online booking link exists but is buried
- Instagram looks better than website
- Strong reviews but weak website
- Good photos but poor layout
- Clear niche but generic homepage copy
- Mobile site has obvious issues
- Services are valuable but hard to understand

## Red Flags

- No website
- Facebook-only presence
- Very old business with no digital activity
- No clear booking/inquiry path
- Already polished agency-built site
- Corporate/franchise brand with centralized marketing
- Business appears closed or inactive

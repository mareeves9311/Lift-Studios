# Local Content Growth Kit

Website, Social, and Monthly Content Support for Local Businesses

Prepared by Megan Reeves

## What This Is

Local Content Growth Kit helps local businesses turn their website and social media into a stronger first impression.

Most local businesses do not need a huge agency. They need:

- A clearer website
- Better social content ideas
- More trust signals
- A stronger reason for someone to book, visit, or buy
- A consistent monthly content system

This service gives local businesses the strategy, creative direction, and content plan they need without making the owner figure it all out from scratch.

## What I Review In A Mini-Audit

For each business, I look at:

- Website first impression
- Homepage clarity
- Mobile flow
- Booking, order, or contact path
- Social media bio and pinned posts
- Content mix
- Trust signals
- Local discovery opportunities
- Quick wins that could improve conversion

The mini-audit is intentionally practical. It focuses on what a real customer sees before deciding whether to book, visit, order, or inquire.

## Sample Brand + Content Kit 1: Made-Up Local Cafe

Business name:
Maple & Main Cafe

Concept:
A cozy neighborhood cafe built around coffee, breakfast, lunch, and weekend brunch.

Audience:
Local professionals, college students, moms after school drop-off, brunch groups, and people looking for a better coffee stop than a chain.

### Logo Direction

Primary logo idea:
Simple serif wordmark reading "Maple & Main" with a small hand-drawn coffee cup or maple leaf mark.

Secondary logo:
Round stamp mark for cups, stickers, menu headers, and Instagram profile image.

Suggested tagline:
Coffee, brunch, and neighborhood mornings.

### Branding Kit

Brand personality:
Warm, familiar, polished, local, comforting, and slightly nostalgic.

Color palette:
- Maple brown: #6B3F2A
- Cream: #F7EFE2
- Soft green: #7A8F68
- Warm gold: #D7A84F
- Charcoal: #2B2B2B

Font direction:
- Logo/headlines: warm editorial serif
- Body copy: clean sans serif
- Accent: handwritten script used very sparingly

Visual style:
- Coffee pours
- Table spreads
- Morning light
- Hands holding mugs
- Menu close-ups
- Exterior/local street shots

### Social Content Examples

Hook:
"I found a cozy little cafe for coffee, brunch, and slow mornings."

Visuals:
Exterior, coffee pour, pastry case, table spread, first sip.

Reel 2: What To Order First

Hook:
"First time at Maple & Main? Here is what I would order."

Visuals:
Menu close-up, latte, breakfast sandwich, pancake stack, final table spread.

CTA:
"Send this to your brunch person."

Carousel 1:
First Time Here? Start With These 5 Orders

4. Best brunch order

Carousel 2:

Slides:

6. Save and send

### Homepage Layout Example

Hero:
Maple & Main Cafe

## Sample Brand + Content Kit 2: Made-Up Local Med Spa

Business name:
Luna Skin Studio

Concept:
A calm, modern med spa focused on subtle, confidence-building skin and injectable treatments.

Audience:
Women ages 28-55 who want expert guidance, natural-looking results, and a provider they can trust.

### Logo Direction

Primary logo idea:
Elegant sans serif wordmark reading "Luna Skin Studio" with a minimal crescent or soft line icon.

Secondary logo:
Minimal "LSS" monogram for profile images, appointment cards, and treatment room signage.

Suggested tagline:
Subtle treatments. Clear guidance. Skin that still feels like you.

### Branding Kit

Brand personality:
Calm, clinical but warm, polished, trustworthy, modern, and confidence-focused.

Color palette:
- Soft ivory: #F8F4EF
- Clay rose: #B98272
- Deep taupe: #5D514B
- Sage gray: #A9B2A4
- Ink: #1F1F1F

Font direction:
- Logo/headlines: refined modern serif or clean luxury sans serif
- Body copy: clear sans serif
- Accent: all-caps small labels for treatment categories

Visual style:
- Treatment room details
- Provider intros
- Product shelf shots
- Consultation moments
- Clean before/after framing where compliant
- Calm neutral backgrounds

### Social Content Examples

Reel 1: Nervous To Book

Hook:
"If you have been nervous to book your first med spa appointment, start here."

Visuals:
Exterior, calm front desk, consultation moment, provider explaining options, treatment room detail.

CTA:
"Book a consultation if you are not sure what to choose."

Reel 2: Treatment Matchmaker

Hook:
"Not sure what to book? Here is where I would start based on your goal."

Body:

- Fine lines: neurotoxin consult
- Dullness: facial or peel
- Volume loss: filler consultation

CTA:
"Save this before your first appointment."

Carousel 1:
New Here? Start With These 3 Appointment Types

Slides:
1. Cover
2. Consultation
3. Skin treatment
4. Injectable consult
5. How to book

Carousel 2:
Botox vs. Filler: Simple Breakdown

Slides:
1. Cover

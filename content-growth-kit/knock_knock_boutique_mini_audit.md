# Knock Knock Boutique Mini-Audit

Business: Knock Knock Boutique  
Website: https://shopknockknock.com/  
Category: Boutique / Jewelry / Accessories  
Locations: Hershey and Elizabethtown, PA  
Contact: knockknockboutique@gmail.com  
Date: June 10, 2026

## First Impression

Knock Knock has a very strong raw brand foundation: whimsical, sparkly, playful, family-owned, and rooted in the Hershey/Elizabethtown shopping experience.

This is not a "brand has no personality" prospect. It is the opposite. The opportunity is to package the existing personality into clearer shopping paths and a repeatable content system.

The strongest current brand assets are:

- "Shop. Sparkle. Conquer."
- Jewel Box product/persona concept
- Permanent jewelry offering
- Family-owned founder story
- Two shop locations
- Customer reviews
- Blog content
- Style quiz
- Events and VIP moments

## What Is Working

1. The brand has a distinctive voice.
   - Phrases like "whimsical pocket of sparkle and wonder," "accessories for every adventure," and "Fancy or fanciful" create a clear personality.

2. The Jewel Box concept is content-friendly.
   - Product names like The Trailblazer, The Professional, The Trendsetter, and The Socialite naturally lend themselves to social content, styling guides, quizzes, and gift guides.

3. Permanent jewelry gives the business an experience-based offer.
   - This is not just product sales. Permanent jewelry can drive appointments, events, bridal parties, birthdays, business events, fundraisers, and friendship/partner experiences.

4. The About page gives the brand heart.
   - The site explains that Knock Knock was founded by Emily Drobnock, a fourth generation business owner, and that the boutique is family-supported.

5. Testimonials are already available.
   - Reviews praise product quality, uniqueness, customer service, and the boutique's one-of-a-kind feel.

6. The brand has multiple conversion paths.
   - Shop Jewel Box
   - Shop accessories
   - Visit Hershey
   - Visit Elizabethtown
   - Book permanent jewelry
   - Take the style quiz
   - Join the newsletter
   - Attend events

## Main Opportunity

The brand has charm, but the customer journey could be clearer.

A new visitor should immediately understand:

- What should I shop first?
- What is Jewel Box?
- What makes permanent jewelry worth booking?
- Which location should I visit?
- What is the style quiz for?
- What makes Knock Knock different from another jewelry/accessory shop?

Right now, those pieces exist, but they could be organized into stronger "start here" paths.

## Website Quick Wins

1. Replace visible "Test Menu" language.
   - This makes the site feel unfinished and can reduce trust.

2. Fix small copy errors.
   - Example: "Unprepardness" should be corrected.

3. Turn the homepage into a clearer shopping pathway.
   - Suggested top-level paths:
   - Shop Jewel Box
   - Book Permanent Jewelry
   - Visit Our Shops
   - Take The Style Quiz

4. Make the Jewel Box personas more useful.
   - The Trailblazer, Professional, Trendsetter, and Socialite should become style pathways, gift guides, and social content series.

5. Give permanent jewelry a stronger visual and booking path.
   - It should feel like an experience, not just an informational page.

6. Use review language closer to the shopping decision.
   - Customer quotes about quality, uniqueness, and service should appear near product/shop CTAs.

7. Clarify the Hershey/Elizabethtown experience.
   - The site should help visitors understand what to expect at each shop and why to visit in person.

Create a **sample work showcase** for **Morning Fuel** — a real independent breakfast and brunch restaurant located at 4890 Carlisle Pike, Mechanicsburg, PA. This is a Lift Studio client deliverable that demonstrates what their brand, website, and social presence could look like with professional content direction. Every design decision should feel specific to Morning Fuel — not a template.

**About the Business**
Name: Morning Fuel
Category: Breakfast, brunch, specialty coffee
Location: Mechanicsburg, PA (Carlisle Pike)
Differentiator: Specialty Guatemalan coffee · Bold chef-driven breakfast menu · Signature dishes including tres leches pancakes, chilaquiles, berry crepe, chorizo bowl
Audience: Local Mechanicsburg residents, weekday coffee regulars, weekend brunch groups, people exploring the area
Conversion goal: Get new visitors to come in for the first time and return as regulars
Current problem: The website opens with a location-selection screen before showing any food, story, or reason to care. The Mechanicsburg menu page is a 404. The social presence doesn't reflect the quality or personality of the in-person experience.

**Recommended Brand Direction**
Morning Fuel already has a name, logo, and vibe. The direction is to amplify what they already have — not replace it.
Primary tone: Warm, bold, neighborhood energy. The kind of place locals text each other about.
Color direction: Deep charcoal or near-black · Warm amber/golden accent (coffee, morning light) · Off-white or cream background
Font direction: Bold, confident sans-serif for headlines · Clean readable sans-serif for body · Nothing trendy or delicate — this is a bold breakfast spot
Visual style: Warm amber morning light · Ceramic dishware · Worn wooden tables · Candid food and people moments · Film grain · Never corporate

**Deliverables to create:**

**1. Homepage Hero Concept**
Show what their website's first impression could look like — replacing the current location-selector with a real brand moment.
Headline: The kind of breakfast that makes you a regular.
Subtext: Specialty Guatemalan coffee and a bold brunch menu in Mechanicsburg. Mon–Sat 7–2:30 · Sun 8–3.
CTA buttons: [Order Online] and [View Menu]
Visual direction: Full-bleed warm food or cafe ambiance image, amber tones, confident typography overlay
Leave one large image slot labeled: [Drop Morning Fuel hero photo here]

**2. Three Instagram Post Mockups in phone frames**
Use Morning Fuel's warm amber/charcoal color direction throughout.

Post 1 — First-time visitor:
Hook: "First time at Morning Fuel? Here's exactly what to order."
Visual: Clean graphic or food flat lay with 3 dish callouts — Chilaquiles, Tres Leches Pancakes, Berry Crepe
CTA: "Save this before you go."

Post 2 — Guatemalan coffee story:
Hook: "Not your average coffee stop. Here's what makes ours different."
Visual: Close crop of a pour-over or latte in a ceramic mug, warm morning light
CTA: "Come in for the coffee. Stay for brunch."

Post 3 — Local discovery:
Hook: "POV: you finally found your Mechanicsburg brunch spot."
Visual: Table spread with full plates, drinks, candid group energy
CTA: "Tag someone who needs to know about this."

**3. Monthly Content Bucket Card**
Header: What 30 days of content looks like for Morning Fuel.
Week 1: Menu cravings — make specific dishes easy to crave before someone arrives
Week 2: Coffee + drinks — own the morning fuel idea beyond just food
Week 3: Local discovery — help new Mechanicsburg customers find and remember them
Week 4: Social proof + experience — reduce hesitation for first-time visitors
Deliverables at bottom: UGC scripts · Carousel concepts · Caption hooks · Content calendar · Bio + pinned post recommendations

**4. Quick Wins Card**
Header: Where we start.
Win 1: Fix the homepage — replace the location selector with a headline, hero image, and CTA that earns attention before asking for a decision
Win 2: Repair the Mechanicsburg menu page (currently 404) and add hours, address, and order button in one clear block
Win 3: Build three pinned posts — "Start Here," "The Coffee Story," and "Customer Favorites" — so every new profile visitor has a clear next step

**Overall design direction:**
Warm, bold, neighborhood energy throughout. Think: a great food magazine spread meets a local restaurant that doesn't take itself too seriously. Leave clearly labeled image slots on the homepage hero and all three Instagram posts — real Morning Fuel photos or Midjourney-generated images will be dropped in. Every page should feel like it was made for this specific business. A Morning Fuel owner should look at this and immediately think: this is us, but better.
